Motivation

21.04.2016

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

Pleasure, passion, and purpose are three types of happiness that vary in sustainability.
Pleasure is easily attained but not long lasting. It is normal to habituate to something that brings you pleasure and return to a default level of happiness not long after.
Passion is harder to come by, but if found, can bring someone a more sustainable sense of happiness.
Finally, purpose is the feeling of contributing to something bigger than ones self, and can bring a sense of happiness that is long lasting.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise, What are your core values?

I struggle with the idea that people have core values that are static and unchanging. I think a persons values are contextual and can change with experience and education. I also struggled to distinguish between what i thought i should value and what i actually value. But I had a go anyway!
Generally or perhaps just in this moment, I value diversity, kindness, open-mindedness and consideration.

What do you stand for?

The general "ness" of what i stand for is the happiness and wellbeing of the many, rather than the few.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

SIY discusses resilience as having three levels: inner calm, emotional resilience, and cognitive resilience.
Inner calm and emotional resilience seem to be connected, and reliant on a good understanding and practice of mindfulness. This is something that I am still working on.
Cognitive resilience is a healthy level of optimism and the ability to take an objective perspective of your successes and failures. I this something that I am already pretty good at.
